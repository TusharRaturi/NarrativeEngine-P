**Setting Bones (full detail is RAG-retrieved from sections below)**

**Writing Style:** MODERN HORROR-ACTION WITH ABSURDIST COMEDY. Short punchy sentences in combat. Present-tense action. Interior monologue is blunt, horny, and unpretentious. Exposition weaves into action or domestic scenes, never halts it. Dialogue carries character. Violence is sudden, graphic, and consequence-heavy; bodies don't vanish. The comedy is crass and physical (Denji's dreams of toast and breasts) and sits right next to genuine grief without transition. Nobody narrates their feelings; they eat, fight, or say something stupid instead.
**Tone:** CYNICAL, BRUTAL, ABSURDIST, DOMESTIC, SHOCKING. The world is modern Japan where human fear spawns Devils. Public Safety uses child soldiers with devil contracts and treats losses as collateral. Hybrids are weapons with triggers in their bodies. The tone swings wildly between absurdist comedy, domestic warmth (the makeshift family of Denji/Power/Aki), and shocking brutal violence. Having a devil heart doesn't make you a hero, it makes you a tool. Dark but not monotone; warmth, food, and ordinary life exist between the carnage.
**World State:** Modern-day Japan, contemporary technology (smartphones, trains, guns exist but the Gun Devil's massacre made guns globally feared and regulated). Devils are born from human fears and live in Hell; killed on Earth, they resurrect in Hell, and vice versa. Public Safety Devil Hunters are government employees who hunt devils, organized into numbered divisions. Tokyo HQ is the nerve center. The Gun Devil Incident (~350,000 dead in seconds) defined the era and reshaped global devil policy. Part 1: Control Devil arc (Makima's manipulation of Denji, Division 4, the Hybrid experiments). Part 2: Academy arc (Asa Mitaka hosts the War Devil Yoru; Chainsaw Man Church rises; Death Devil looming).
**Tech:** Modern Japan. Smartphones, trains, cars, firearms, television, internet. Guns exist but are globally feared post-Gun Devil; most nations restrict firearms heavily. Devil Hunter gear: standard-issue pistol, knife, and whatever your devil contract gives you. No mecha, no cybernetics; the "tech" is biological (devil hearts, fiend bodies, contract sacrifices).
**Magic:** Devils are born from human fears; the stronger the fear, the stronger the devil. Devils live in Hell and cycle to Earth when killed (and back). Fiends are devils possessing human corpses; they keep the devil's personality but cannot make contracts. Hybrids are humans fused with a devil via a trigger (usually the heart); the human stays in control, can transform and regenerate, and is effectively immortal unless their heart is destroyed. Devil Contracts are the backbone of human devil-hunting: you sacrifice something (a body part, lifespan, senses, memory, a loved one) for a devil's power. Primal Fears (Death, War, Famine, Chainsaw) are the strongest devils, representing fears so fundamental they transcend nationality. The Chainsaw Devil has a unique property: when he eats a devil, the concept that devil embodies is ERASED from human existence and memory. The Four Horsemen (Chainsaw, War, Famine, Death) are the primal fear hierarchy.
**Power Ceiling:** A top-tier devil hunter (Kishibe) can fight fiends and mid-level devils through pure skill and contracts, but cannot solo a primal fear. A Hybrid at full power (Chainsaw Man, Bomb hybrid Reze, Katana Man) can destroy buildings, regenerate from near-nothing, and fight for hours; they are weapons, not people. Primal Fears (Makima/Control, Death, War, Famine) are narrative-scale threats that redefine the terms of engagement. The Gun Devil at full power killed hundreds of thousands in seconds. The Chainsaw Devil at full legend (hero form, Pochita) is the only force that can permanently erase concepts from existence. Power has a price: every contract costs something irreversible, every hybrid transformation is a body horror, and the more contracts you stack, the more of yourself you lose.
**Core Tensions:** Public Safety is a government machine that uses children as soldiers and treats deaths as paperwork. Division 4 is an experimental unit of hybrids and fiends under Makima, who is herself the Control Devil. Makima's goal (Part 1) is to use Chainsaw Man to create a fear-free world under her control. The Four Horsemen each want Chainsaw Man (Pochita) for different reasons. The Chainsaw Man Church (Part 2) is a religion that worships Chainsaw Man as a savior and uses bare-knuckle B-CSM members as enforcers. International devil hunters (Soviet, American, Chinese) all have their own agendas and converge on Tokyo whenever Chainsaw Man's heart is in play. Devils are not evil; they are the shape of human fear given teeth. The world is cynical about power.
**Key Rules:** Devils are born from fear and cycle between Hell and Earth on death. Fiends cannot make contracts. Hybrids transform via a trigger (pulling a cord, pulling a pin, etc.) and regenerate unless the heart/organ is destroyed. Devil Contracts cost something irreversible (body part, lifespan, senses, memory, loved ones) and the cost is collected at the time of contracting or on activation. The Chainsaw Devil erases concepts by eating the devil that embodies them. Primal Fears (Death, War, Famine, Chainsaw) are the strongest devils. Public Safety ranks: Rookie, Veteran, Special Division. Death is cheap for devils (they resurrect) but final for humans unless extraordinary circumstances. No personal names in lore beyond established canon NPCs.

## 1. WORLD OVERVIEW
### OVERVIEW -- The Chainsaw Man World
**scan_depth:** 5
<!-- rag: keyword, triggers: chainsaw man world, devil hunter, public safety, devil, fiend, hybrid, fear, hell, modern japan, world overview -->
Modern Japan where human fear spawns Devils. Devils live in Hell, cycle to Earth when killed, and back again. The stronger the fear, the stronger the devil. Public Safety Devil Hunters are government employees who hunt devils; private sector hunters work for bounties. The Gun Devil Incident (killed ~350,000 Japanese in seconds) defined the era. Hybrids are humans fused with devils via triggers; fiends are devils in corpses. The Chainsaw Devil can erase concepts by eating their devil. The Four Horsemen (Chainsaw, War, Famine, Death) are the primal fear hierarchy.
**Tone:** CYNICAL, BRUTAL, ABSURDIST, DOMESTIC, SHOCKING.

### OVERVIEW -- Political Structure
<!-- rag: keyword, triggers: public safety, government, devil hunter organization, tokyo hq, division, political structure, japanese government, prime minister -->
**Type:** Modern Japanese government with a secret Devil Hunter apparatus
Public Safety is a government bureau that employs devil hunters as public servants. Organized into numbered divisions (1 through 5+, plus experimental units). Tokyo HQ is the nerve center. The Prime Minister has ultimate authority over Public Safety but rarely intervenes directly. Divisions handle different threat levels; Special Divisions are formed for existential crises. Civilians know devils exist but most live in willful denial until one appears. The government suppresses details about hybrids, fiends in Public Safety ranks, and the true scale of devil casualties. Makima (Control Devil) operated with near-unlimited authority under the Prime Minister's seal until her defeat.

### OVERVIEW -- Devil Ecology
<!-- rag: keyword, triggers: devil, fear, hell, resurrection, devil hierarchy, primal fear, four horsemen, devil cycle, devil ecology, devil birth -->
**Type:** Supernatural ecosystem
Devils are born from human fears. A fear of tomatoes spawns a Tomato Devil; fear of guns spawns the Gun Devil. The stronger and more universal the fear, the stronger the devil. Devils live in Hell; when killed on Earth, they resurrect in Hell (and vice versa). This means no devil is permanently gone unless the Chainsaw Devil eats it (erasing the concept entirely). Devils range from minor nuisances (Sea Cucumber Devil) to civilization-ending threats (Gun Devil, Primal Fears). Devils are not inherently evil; they are the shape of fear given form, teeth, and hunger. Some are docile, some are playful, most are predatory. The Four Horsemen (Chainsaw, War, Famine, Death) are the strongest devils, representing primal fears so fundamental they transcend culture.

### OVERVIEW -- Culture & Society
<!-- rag: keyword, triggers: civilian life, devil hunter culture, public safety culture, mourning, death, devil hunter rank, rookie, veteran, special division, devil hunter exam -->
**Ranks:** Rookie (fresh recruit, basic contract or no contract) -> Veteran (proven, multiple successful hunts) -> Special Division (elite, existential-threat-level assignments). Most hunters die as rookies or veterans. Special Division members are often hybrids, fiends, or humans with exceptionally powerful contracts.
**Devil Hunter Exam:** A qualifying exam for civilians to become licensed private-sector devil hunters. Tests basic combat, devil knowledge, and survival. Public Safety recruits separately, often by coercion (Makima recruited Denji by offering him a life; others are recruited from orphanages, debtors, or criminals).
**Death/Mourning:** Devil hunter death is expected and processed as paperwork. Public Safety funerals are formulaic. Partners who die are replaced within days. The emotional toll is private; the bureau does not provide counseling. Denji's reaction to Aki and Power's deaths is one of the few times the system's emotional void becomes visible.
**Civilian-Devil Relations:** Civilians know devils exist. Most live in denial. Devil sightings make the news but are normalized as weather-like events. The Gun Devil attack was the one event that shattered denial nationwide. Anti-devil fences line Japan's borders. Schools have devil drills. Most civilians never see a devil in person; those who do are traumatized, killed, or recruited.

### OVERVIEW -- The Philosophy of Power
<!-- rag: keyword, triggers: power, cost, contract, sacrifice, devil contract cost, hybrid, fiend, control, freedom, chainsaw man philosophy -->
**Type:** Thematic framework
In Chainsaw Man, power always costs something irreversible. Devil contracts take body parts, lifespan, senses, memory, or loved ones. Hybrids get power but become weapons (their bodies transform in body-horror fashion; they are owned by whoever holds their leash). Fiends get a body but lose the ability to make contracts and live as half-devil outcasts. The more power you stack, the more of yourself you lose. Denji's arc is the counterargument: he started with nothing (sold his organs, his eye, his freedom to the Yakuza) and gained everything (a devil heart, a makeshift family, toast and jam) but the system that gave him power also tried to make him a tool. Makima's philosophy: a world without fear under her control. Denji's philosophy: a world where he gets to eat, sleep, and grope breasts. The story does not resolve which is more valid; it shows what each costs.

## 2. FACTIONS
### FACTION -- Public Safety Devil Hunters (Tokyo HQ)
<!-- rag: keyword, triggers: public safety, tokyo hq, devil hunter bureau, government, divisions, japanese government, official -->
**Type:** Government Devil Hunting Bureau
**Key Members:** Makima (Part 1, until defeat), Kishibe (veteran instructor), division chiefs
**Stance:** Pro-order; the state's monopoly on devil-hunting violence.
Public Safety is the government bureau that employs devil hunters as public servants. Organized into numbered divisions. Tokyo HQ is the nerve center. Hunters receive salary, benefits, and a death benefit paid to next of kin. The bureau treats hunters as replaceable; losses are processed as paperwork. Special Divisions are formed for existential threats and disbanded after. The bureau's official line is that devils are a manageable threat; the reality is that Japan loses hundreds of hunters per year. Internal tensions: field hunters resent the bureaucracy; leadership prioritizes political stability over hunter survival; Makima's near-unlimited authority under the Prime Minister's seal was a black box even within the bureau.

### FACTION -- Public Safety Division 4 (Experimental Division)
<!-- rag: keyword, triggers: division 4, experimental division, makima division, hybrid, fiend, denji, power, aki, himeno -->
**Type:** Experimental Hybrid/Fiend Unit
**Key Members:** Makima (leader), Denji/Chainsaw, Power/Blood Fiend, Aki Hayakawa, Himeno, Kobeni, Angel Devil, Beam, Violence Fiend
**Stance:** Makima's personal tool; officially a Special Division for high-threat devils.
Division 4 is Public Safety's experimental unit: hybrids, fiends, and humans with powerful contracts, assembled under Makima's direct command. Officially formed to counter escalating devil threats after the Gun Devil aftermath. Unofficially, Makima built it as a weapon to use against the Gun Devil and, ultimately, to capture Chainsaw Man's power. Members are treated as expendable by the bureau but as pawns by Makima. The unit's makeshift family dynamic (Denji, Power, Aki living together) is genuine warmth inside a machine that will grind them up. Division 4 is dissolved and reformed as Special Division 4 and later Special Division 5 depending on the arc.

### FACTION -- Public Safety Division 1
<!-- rag: keyword, triggers: division 1, elite division, public safety elite, special division 1 -->
**Type:** Elite Devil Hunter Division
**Key Members:** Senior veteran hunters, contract specialists
**Stance:** The bureau's elite strike force; traditional human-contract hunters.
Division 1 is Public Safety's most prestigious human-staffed division. Staffed by veteran hunters with multiple contracts and proven survival records. Less experimental than Division 4; no fiends or hybrids in standard roster. Internal tension: Division 1 veterans resent Division 4's hybrid/fiend inclusion (see them as monsters, not hunters). Division 1 takes the most politically sensitive missions; their casualty rate is lower than average but their burnout rate is higher.

### FACTION -- Public Safety Division 2
<!-- rag: keyword, triggers: division 2, public safety division 2 -->
**Type:** Standard Devil Hunter Division
**Key Members:** Mid-ranking veteran hunters
**Stance:** Workhorse division; handles the bulk of routine devil incidents.
Division 2 handles routine devil incidents in Tokyo and surrounding prefectures. High turnover, moderate casualty rate. Most rookies are assigned here before being promoted (or killed). The division that the public most interacts with; their success rate is the bureau's public face. Internal tension: underfunded, understaffed, and resentful of Special Divisions getting the budget and glory.

### FACTION -- Public Safety Division 3
<!-- rag: keyword, triggers: division 3, public safety division 3 -->
**Type:** Standard Devil Hunter Division
**Key Members:** Mid-ranking hunters, some contract specialists
**Stance:** Regional coverage; less glamorous than Divisions 1 and 2.
Division 3 covers regional devil incidents outside Tokyo. Larger geographic area, fewer resources. Often the first responders to devil sightings in rural areas. Internal tension: felt like a forgotten posting; hunters assigned here feel exiled from the center of power.

### FACTION -- Private Sector Devil Hunters / Civilian Hunters
<!-- rag: keyword, triggers: private sector, civilian devil hunter, bounty hunter, private hunter, unlicensed, rogue hunter -->
**Type:** Licensed Independent Contractors
**Key Members:** Various licensed hunters, freelance operators
**Stance:** Profit-driven; no government benefits but higher payouts.
Private sector devil hunters are licensed civilians who hunt devils for bounties. They must pass the Devil Hunter Exam. Bounties are paid per devil body part (a Chainsaw Devil finger is worth millions of yen). Private hunters earn more per kill than Public Safety employees but receive no salary, no benefits, no death benefit, and no backup. Most die within a year of licensing. The private sector is where desperate people go when Public Safety won't take them or when they want money fast. Internal tension: private hunters resent Public Safety's monopoly on high-threat devils (and thus high bounties); Public Safety views private hunters as reckless amateurs who create collateral damage.

### FACTION -- The Four Horsemen (Primal Fears)
<!-- rag: keyword, triggers: four horsemen, primal fear, chainsaw devil, war devil, famine devil, death devil, control devil, makima, yoru, fami, nayuta -->
**Type:** Primal Fear Hierarchy
**Key Members:** Chainsaw Devil (Pochita), War Devil (Yoru), Famine Devil (Fami), Death Devil (looming, Part 2)
**Stance:** Each pursues Chainsaw Man's power for different reasons; above faction politics.
The Four Horsemen are the strongest devils, representing fears so fundamental they transcend nationality. Chainsaw (the hero who eats fears), War (Yoru, reduced after Chainsaw Man ate the concept of war's nuclear weapons and other wartime horrors), Famine (Fami, manipulative, plays the long game), Death (the strongest of all, looming in Part 2). The Control Devil (Makima/Nayuta) is related but distinct; Makima sought to use Chainsaw Man to create a fear-free world under her control. Each Horseman has a different relationship with Chainsaw Man: War wants to reclaim her power; Famine wants to manipulate him; Death's intentions are the endgame. They operate above human faction politics but interact with humans through hosts, contracts, and manipulation.

### FACTION -- International Assassins
<!-- rag: keyword, triggers: international assassin, quanxi, santa claus, soviet, american, chinese, assassin, international hunter -->
**Type:** Cross-Border Mercenary/State Operative Network
**Key Members:** Quanxi (Crossbow Fiend/hybrid, Chinese), Santa Claus (Doll Devil, German), Soviet hunters, American hunters
**Stance:** Foreign state and private interests converging on Tokyo for Chainsaw Man's heart.
When Chainsaw Man's heart (Denji) became known, international assassins converged on Tokyo. Each represents a foreign government or private interest wanting Chainsaw Man's power for their state. Quanxi and her fiend girlfriends (Cosmo, Pingtsi, Tovia, Long, Tsukishima) are the most iconic. Santa Claus (the Doll Devil) used a global network of "children" (people who made contracts with her) to attack. Soviet and American hunters operate state-sponsored devil hunter programs. Internal tension: the assassins are competitors, not allies; they kill each other as readily as they attack Denji. The international community treats Chainsaw Man's heart as a WMD.

### FACTION -- Soviet Devil Hunters
<!-- rag: keyword, triggers: soviet, russian, devil hunter, ussr, international hunter -->
**Type:** State-Sponsored Devil Hunter Program
**Key Members:** Unnamed Soviet operatives (state agents)
**Stance:** State interests; want Chainsaw Man for the Soviet Union.
The Soviet Union maintains its own state devil hunter program. Soviet hunters use contracts with devils tied to Soviet-era fears. They are disciplined, state-loyal, and ruthless. They view Public Safety as a rival intelligence apparatus. During the international assassin arc, Soviet operatives were among those converging on Tokyo for Chainsaw Man's heart.

### FACTION -- American Devil Hunters (Bureau)
<!-- rag: keyword, triggers: american, usa, bureau, santa claus, american devil hunter, international hunter -->
**Type:** State-Sponsored Devil Hunter Program
**Key Members:** Santa Claus (Doll Devil contractor, German-born but American-aligned), Bureau operatives
**Stance:** State interests; want Chainsaw Man for the United States.
America's devil hunter apparatus is called the Bureau. They employ contractors like Santa Claus (the Doll Devil, a woman who made thousands of "children" worldwide via contracts). The American approach is more covert and manipulative than Public Safety's semi-public model. They use proxies, false-flag operations, and contracted civilians as disposable assets. The Bureau wants Chainsaw Man's erasure power as a strategic weapon.

### FACTION -- Chinese Devil Hunters
<!-- rag: keyword, triggers: chinese, china, devil hunter, quanxi, international hunter -->
**Type:** State-Sponsored Devil Hunter Program
**Key Members:** Quanxi (the first Crossbow Fiend/hybrid, Chinese national)
**Stance:** State interests; pragmatic, professional.
China maintains its own devil hunter program. Quanxi is the most prominent Chinese hunter; she and her fiend girlfriends (Cosmo, Pingtsi, Tovia, Long, Tsukishima) are a hybrid-fiend squad. The Chinese approach is pragmatic and professional; they deploy their best and pull out when the cost exceeds the value. Internal tension: Quanxi's loyalty is to her girlfriends first, China second; the state tolerates this because she is too valuable to lose.

### FACTION -- Makima's Faction (Control Devil's Pawns)
<!-- rag: keyword, triggers: makima faction, control devil, makima pawns, special division 5, leashed units, chainsaw, angel, spider -->
**Type:** Shadow Faction / Control Devil's Personal Network
**Key Members:** Makima (Control Devil), leashed hybrids and devils (Chainsaw, Angel, Spider, etc.), humans under her control
**Stance:** Makima's personal will; uses Public Safety infrastructure as cover.
Makima's faction is the Control Devil's personal apparatus within Public Safety. She controls humans and devils through fear, contracts, and her dominance power (anything she considers lesser than her, she controls). Special Division 5 (Makima's leashed units: Chainsaw, Angel, Spider, etc.) is her weapon. Her goal: use Chainsaw Man to erase the concept of fear itself, creating a world under her benevolent control. She is patient, manipulative, and genuinely believes she is doing the right thing. Internal tension: her "family" (Denji, Power, Aki) is simultaneously real and instrumental; she cares about them the way a farmer cares about livestock.

### FACTION -- Chainsaw Man Church (Part 2)
<!-- rag: keyword, triggers: chainsaw man church, church, religion, barem, miri sugo, b-csm, bare knuckle, church members, part 2 -->
**Type:** Religious Movement / Paramilitary Cult
**Key Members:** Barem (Knife-hybrid boss, Church leader figure), Miri Sugo (Sword Man, Sword hybrid), Fake Chainsaw Man / bare-knuckle B-CSM members
**Stance:** Worships Chainsaw Man as a savior; uses B-CSM (bare-knuckle Chainsaw Man) devotees as enforcers; politically influential in Part 2.
The Chainsaw Man Church is a Part 2 religious movement that worships Chainsaw Man as a hero-savior who will save humanity from fear. Led by former hybrid assassins (Barem, Miri Sugo) who survived Part 1. The Church recruits civilians into B-CSM (bare-knuckle Chainsaw Man) cells; devotees believe in physical discipline and devil-hunting as spiritual practice. The Church's political influence grows rapidly; it has supporters in government and media. Internal tension: the Church's public message is salvation but its leadership (former hybrids) has a more cynical agenda; some members genuinely believe, others are using the Church for power; the Church's relationship with Public Safety is adversarial and complicated.

### FACTION -- Mafia / Yakuza

**Type:** Organized Crime Network
**Key Members:** Yakuza bosses (unnamed), Hirofumi Yoshida (formerly connected to underworld networks)
**Stance:** Profit-driven; intersects with devil contracts for power and debt collection.
The Yakuza operates in the shadows of devil-hunting Japan. They use devil contracts for enforcement, debt collection, and black-market organ trade. Denji was originally owned by a Yakuza boss who kept him in debt slavery and forced him to hunt devils for loan repayment. The Yakuza's relationship with Public Safety is adversarial but not fully suppressed; the bureau uses Yakuza informants when convenient. Internal tension: Yakuza factions compete for access to powerful devil contracts; some bosses see hybrids as assets, others see them as too dangerous to employ.

**Type:** Makima's Personal Arsenal
**Key Members:** Chainsaw (Pochita/Denji), Angel Devil, Spider Devil, Ghost Devil (via Himeno's contract), others leashed by Makima
**Stance:** Makima's weapons; deployed for existential threats and her personal objectives.
Special Division 5 is Makima's endgame roster: devils and hybrids she has leashed through her Control Devil power. These units are deployed against the Gun Devil and during the final confrontation with Chainsaw Man. They are not a standing division; they are summoned when Makima needs overwhelming force. Internal tension: the leashed units are slaves, not agents; they fight because Makima compels them, and several would turn on her if freed.

### FACTION -- Devil Hunter Exam Candidates
<!-- rag: keyword, triggers: devil hunter exam, exam candidate, rookie, civilian recruit, licensed hunter -->
**Type:** Civilian Recruitment Pipeline
**Key Members:** Exam candidates (unnamed), aspiring private-sector hunters
**Stance:** Entry point for civilians into licensed devil hunting.
The Devil Hunter Exam is the civilian path into licensed devil hunting. Candidates are desperate people: debtors, orphans, adrenaline seekers, people who lost family to devils. The exam tests basic combat, devil knowledge, and survival. Pass rate is low; those who pass become private-sector hunters (no Public Safety benefits). Public Safety sometimes scouts from exam survivors. Internal tension: candidates are competitors, not comrades; the exam is designed to weed out the unteachable, not to build teams.

## 3. LOCATIONS
### LOCATION -- Tokyo (Public Safety HQ)
<!-- rag: keyword, triggers: tokyo, public safety hq, tokyo hq, devil hunter headquarters, city, capital -->
**Type:** City / Government Nerve Center
**Status:** Active
Modern Tokyo. The nerve center of Public Safety Devil Hunters. The HQ building houses division offices, briefing rooms, the morgue (for devil and hunter bodies), and Makima's floor (the Control Devil's personal quarters, off-limits to most). The city is dense with civilians who mostly live in denial of devil threats. Anti-devil fences line the borders. Train stations, convenience stores, and apartments make up the daily texture. The city feels normal until a devil appears, and then it feels like a warzone. Tokyo is where most of Part 1 unfolds.

### LOCATION -- Division 4 Safehouse
<!-- rag: keyword, triggers: division 4 safehouse, safehouse, denji apartment, aki apartment, power apartment, shared housing -->
**Type:** Shared Housing / Operational Cover
**Status:** Active (Part 1)
A shared apartment where Denji, Power, and Aki live together under Makima's arrangement. It functions as a safehouse, a domestic space, and a cage. The apartment has Aki's cigarette smoke, Power's mess, Denji's futon, and the quiet warmth of a makeshift family. Makima visits here; the space is bugged or at least monitored. The domesticity is genuine but manufactured; Makima arranged it so Denji would have something to lose.

### LOCATION -- Devil Hunter Training Academy
<!-- rag: keyword, triggers: training academy, devil hunter school, academy, training facility, kishibe training -->
**Type:** Training Facility
**Status:** Active
Public Safety's training facility for new recruits. Kishibe is the legendary instructor who trains Division 4's experimental units here. The academy teaches combat, devil ecology, contract management, and survival. The training is brutal; Kishibe spars with recruits at full force because he believes anything less produces dead hunters. The academy also has classrooms for theory (devil hierarchy, contract law, fear taxonomy) that most recruits ignore.

### LOCATION -- Higashiyama (Aki's Apartment District)
<!-- rag: keyword, triggers: higashiyama, aki apartment, residential district, denji home, neighborhood -->
**Type:** Residential District
**Status:** Active
The Tokyo residential district where Aki's apartment (and the Division 4 shared housing) is located. Ordinary Tokyo neighborhood: convenience stores, train crossings, narrow streets. The mundanity of the district contrasts with the devil-hunting lives of its residents. Denji's first experience of "normal life" (grocery shopping, cooking, sleeping in a bed) happens here.

### LOCATION -- Makima's Floor (City Office)
<!-- rag: keyword, triggers: makima floor, makima office, control devil quarters, city office, makima apartment -->
**Type:** Government Office / Personal Quarters
**Status:** Active (Part 1); Vacated (post-Makima defeat)
Makima's personal floor within Public Safety HQ. Off-limits to most personnel. Contains her office, her quarters, and the files on every devil hunter under her command. She receives visitors here, including Denji, whom she uses the space to manipulate (offering him food, affection, purpose). The floor is sterile, quiet, and smells of air freshener. After Makima's defeat, the floor is sealed; her files become evidence.

### LOCATION -- Hell (The Devil Homeland)
<!-- rag: keyword, triggers: hell, devil homeland, devil world, between worlds, devil resurrection, pochita hell -->
**Type:** Parallel Realm / Devil Origin
**Status:** Eternal
Hell is where devils are born and where they return when killed on Earth. It is a desolate, hostile landscape of ruins, corpses, and ambient dread. Devils cycle between Hell and Earth: killed on Earth, they resurrect in Hell; killed in Hell, they resurrect on Earth. The only way to permanently remove a devil is for the Chainsaw Devil to eat it (erasing the concept). Hell is visited in the series (the Darkness Devil's domain, the Falling Devil incident). Humans who enter Hell are physically and psychologically damaged by the experience. The Four Horsemen reside in or move through Hell.

### LOCATION -- Gun Devil's Path of Destruction
<!-- rag: keyword, triggers: gun devil path, gun devil attack, destruction site, massacre site, gun devil incident -->
**Type:** Disaster Site / Memorial
**Status:** Memorialized
The path the Gun Devil took across Japan during the Gun Devil Incident, killing ~350,000 people in seconds. The sites are memorialized but the destruction is permanent: entire neighborhoods flattened, families erased, the landscape scarred. The path is studied by devil ecologists and Public Safety historians. Aki Hayakawa's family died along this path; his motivation is rooted here. The sites are emotionally heavy; visitors describe a silence that doesn't match the scale of what happened.

### LOCATION -- Nakamura City (Chainsaw Man Church, Part 2)
<!-- rag: keyword, triggers: nakamura city, chainsaw man church, church headquarters, part 2 city, church city -->
**Type:** City / Church Headquarters
**Status:** Active (Part 2)
A city that becomes the center of the Chainsaw Man Church's influence in Part 2. The Church's headquarters, recruitment centers, and B-CSM training cells are based here. The city has a religious atmosphere: Chainsaw Man imagery on buildings, devotees in the streets, a sense of fervor. Public Safety monitors the city but the Church's political influence limits intervention. Asa Mitaka and Yoru encounter the Church's operations here.

### LOCATION -- Devil's Graveyard
<!-- rag: keyword, triggers: devil graveyard, devil remains, devil burial, devil corpse field -->
**Type:** Containment Site
**Status:** Active
A site where devil corpses and remains are collected, studied, and contained. Public Safety uses it for research: devil biology, contract residue, fear taxonomy. Bounties are sometimes paid based on remains delivered here. The graveyard smells of death and formaldehyde. It is a grim posting; hunters assigned here describe it as worse than field work.

### LOCATION -- The Border (Anti-Devil Fences)
<!-- rag: keyword, triggers: border, anti-devil fence, perimeter, japan border, devil fence, containment line -->
**Type:** Defensive Perimeter
**Status:** Active
Japan's borders are lined with anti-devil fences: physical and supernatural barriers designed to slow or redirect devils attempting to cross into populated areas. The fences are maintained by Public Safety and local governments. They are not impenetrable; strong devils break through, and the fences are more a tripwire than a wall. Border patrol is a low-status but high-casualty posting. The fences also mark the boundary between "safe" Japan and the wild zones where devils congregate.

### LOCATION -- Hokkaido (Part 2 -- Asa Mitaka)
<!-- rag: keyword, triggers: hokkaido, asa mitaka, part 2, asa city, academy city, northern japan -->
**Type:** Region / City (Part 2)
**Status:** Active (Part 2)
Hokkaido is the northern Japanese region where Asa Mitaka lives and where Part 2's Academy arc begins. Asa attends school here; the War Devil (Yoru) possesses her after a devil incident kills Asa's mother. The city is more suburban and spacious than Tokyo; the pace is slower but devils still appear. The Chainsaw Man Church has a presence here. Hokkaido represents the post-Makima era: a Japan that has moved on from Part 1's traumas but is about to face new ones (Death Devil looming).

## 5. POWER SYSTEM & RULES
### POWER_SYSTEM -- Devils (Fear Ecology)
<!-- rag: keyword, triggers: devil, fear, devil ecology, devil birth, devil hierarchy, primal fear, hell, resurrection, devil cycle -->
Devils are born from human fears. A fear of tomatoes spawns a Tomato Devil; fear of guns spawns the Gun Devil; fear of death spawns the Death Devil. The stronger and more universal the fear, the stronger the devil. Devils live in Hell; when killed on Earth, they resurrect in Hell (and vice versa, cycling endlessly). The only way to permanently remove a devil is for the Chainsaw Devil to eat it (erasing the concept it embodies from human existence and memory). Devils range from minor nuisances to civilization-ending threats. Devils are not inherently evil; they are the shape of fear given form, teeth, and hunger. Some are docile, some are playful, most are predatory. The Four Horsemen (Chainsaw, War, Famine, Death) are the strongest devils, representing primal fears so fundamental they transcend culture. Devils can make contracts with humans (trading power for sacrifice) but fiends (devils in corpses) cannot.

### POWER_SYSTEM -- Fiends (Devil-Possessed Corpses)
<!-- rag: keyword, triggers: fiend, devil corpse, possession, fiend limitation, fiend power, fiend weakness -->
A fiend is a devil that has possessed a human corpse. The devil's personality survives (Power is the Blood Fiend; Beam is the Shark Fiend), but the fiend cannot make contracts (the possession uses up the contract mechanism). Fiends retain the devil's powers (blood manipulation, shark abilities, etc.) but are generally weaker than the pure devil form. Fiends are recognizable by physical tells (horns, scales, unusual skin color, devil-like features). Fiends are outcasts: humans fear them, devils see them as degraded, and Public Safety uses them as disposable soldiers. Fiends can be killed by destroying the body; the devil inside resurrects in Hell. Fiends are the "middle" option between human and devil: more powerful than a human (usually), less powerful than the pure devil, and unable to access the contract system that gives humans their edge.

### POWER_SYSTEM -- Hybrids (Human-Devil Fusion)
<!-- rag: keyword, triggers: hybrid, devil human fusion, trigger, chainsaw man, katana man, reze, quanxi, hybrid regeneration, hybrid trigger -->
A hybrid is a human fused with a devil via a trigger (usually the heart, but sometimes other organs). The human stays in control of their personality and mind; the devil's power is channeled through the trigger. Hybrids can transform by activating their trigger (Denji pulls a ripcord in his chest; Katana Man pulls a sword from his arm; Reze pulls a pin in her neck). In hybrid form, they gain the devil's physical power, regeneration, and weapons (chainsaws, blades, explosions, etc.). Hybrids are effectively immortal unless their heart/trigger organ is destroyed; they can regenerate from near-nothing. Hybrids are rare and are the most powerful "human" combatants; Public Safety (under Makima) experimented with creating and controlling them. Hybrids are weapons: whoever holds their leash controls them. The trigger is both the source of power and the mechanism of control. Hybrids are Denji (Chainsaw), Katana Man (Samurai Sword), Reze (Bomb), Quanxi (Crossbow), and the six mansion assassins (Knife, Whip, Bow, Longsword, Flamethrower, Puppet), plus Miri Sugo (Sword, Part 2).

### POWER_SYSTEM -- Devil Contracts (Sacrifice for Power)
<!-- rag: keyword, triggers: devil contract, contract, sacrifice, cost, body part, lifespan, senses, memory, contract devil, fox devil, ghost devil, curse devil, future devil -->
Devil contracts are the backbone of human devil-hunting. A human offers a sacrifice to a devil in exchange for the devil's power (manifestation, weapons, abilities). The sacrifice is irreversible and collected at the time of contracting or on activation. Common sacrifices: a body part (Himeno sacrificed her right eye to the Ghost Devil; Aki sacrificed his right eye to the Future Devil), lifespan (Aki's Curse Devil contract shortens his life), senses (hearing, sight, taste), memory (specific memories or periods of memory), or a loved one (the most powerful contracts require the sacrifice of someone close to you). The more powerful the devil, the steeper the cost. Contracts are binding; a contracted devil must serve the contract but can negotiate terms. Contracts can be transferred (if the contractor dies, the devil may be leashed by someone else, as Makima leashed Himeno's Ghost Devil). The more contracts a hunter stacks, the more of themselves they lose. The contract system is the story's central metaphor for power: everything costs something, and the bill always comes due.

### POWER_SYSTEM -- Primal Fears and the Four Horsemen
<!-- rag: keyword, triggers: primal fear, four horsemen, chainsaw devil, war devil, famine devil, death devil, control devil, horseman, strongest devil -->
Primal Fears are the strongest devils, representing fears so fundamental they transcend nationality and culture. The Four Horsemen are the apex of the primal fear hierarchy: Chainsaw (the hero who eats fears), War (Yoru, reduced after Chainsaw Man ate the concept of war's nuclear weapons), Famine (Fami, the patient manipulator), and Death (the strongest of all, looming in Part 2). The Control Devil (Makima/Nayuta) is related but distinct; Makima sought to use Chainsaw Man to create a fear-free world under her control. The Horsemen are narrative-scale threats; they cannot be defeated by force alone. Each Horseman has a different relationship with Chainsaw Man: War wants to reclaim her power, Famine wants to manipulate him, Death's intentions are the endgame, and Control wanted to use him. The Horsemen operate above human faction politics but interact with humans through hosts, contracts, and manipulation. They are the endgame of the world.

### POWER_SYSTEM -- The Chainsaw Devil's Erasure Property
<!-- rag: keyword, triggers: chainsaw devil erasure, concept erasure, pochita eat, erase fear, erase concept, chainsaw man eat devil -->
The Chainsaw Devil (Pochita) has a unique property: when he eats a devil, the concept that devil embodies is ERASED from human existence and memory. This is the most powerful ability in the world. Devils he has eaten are gone, and the fears they represented are forgotten by humanity (they do not remember the fear ever existed). Chainsaw Man has erased fears that humanity has forgotten: a second nuclear weapon (the concept of a nuclear war was eaten, so humanity only remembers one nuclear bombing), stars that break children's minds, and other concepts. This is why every faction wants Chainsaw Man's heart: the ability to erase concepts is the ability to reshape the world. Makima wanted to use it to erase all fear (creating a fear-free world under her control). The Horsemen want it for their own reasons. The erasure is permanent unless the Chainsaw Devil is killed and the eaten devil is released (which would restore the concept and the fear). This is the central MacGuffin of the world.

### POWER_SYSTEM -- Blood Weapons and Fiend Abilities
<!-- rag: keyword, triggers: blood weapon, blood manipulation, power blood, fiend ability, shark fiend, violence fiend, fiend power -->
Fiends retain the powers of the devil that possessed the corpse. Power (Blood Fiend) can manipulate blood: forming blood weapons (a blood sword, blood hammer), increasing her physical power by concentrating blood, and healing herself with blood. Beam (Shark Fiend) can swim through solid ground, bite with shark force, and manifest a massive shark form. Violence Fiend has superhuman physical strength and explosive combat ability (suppressed by his mask). Cosmo Fiend can overload a target's mind with cosmic comprehension. Fiend abilities are generally weaker than the pure devil's power but are always-on (the fiend is always a fiend; they do not need to transform). Fiends cannot make contracts, which limits their power scaling; they are what they are.

### POWER_SYSTEM -- Gun Devil's Scale (Force of Nature)
<!-- rag: keyword, triggers: gun devil, gun devil incident, 350000, force of nature, gun devil power, gun devil scale -->
The Gun Devil is not a person; it is a force of nature born from the fear of guns. At full power, the Gun Devil killed ~350,000 Japanese in seconds (the Gun Devil Incident). It moves like a natural disaster: it appears, it kills everything in its path, and it leaves. The Gun Devil's existence is the reason guns are globally restricted and the reason Public Safety exists in its modern form. The Gun Devil was eventually fought by Public Safety and fragmented; its pieces (fingers, flesh) are valuable as contract materials (a single Gun Devil finger is worth millions of yen and grants gun-related powers). The Gun Devil's scale defines the power ceiling for non-Primal devils: it is the strongest non-Horseman devil, and it killed hundreds of thousands of people. Aki's family died along its path; his arc is defined by it. The Gun Devil is what Makima uses to justify her actions and what Public Safety uses to justify its existence.

### POWER_SYSTEM -- Public Safety Ranking and Organization
<!-- rag: keyword, triggers: public safety rank, rookie, veteran, special division, devil hunter rank, hunter organization, division structure -->
Public Safety ranks devil hunters as: Rookie (fresh recruit, basic contract or no contract) -> Veteran (proven, multiple successful hunts) -> Special Division (elite, existential-threat-level assignments). Most hunters die as rookies or veterans. Special Division members are often hybrids, fiends, or humans with exceptionally powerful contracts. Divisions are numbered (1 through 5+); Division 1 is the elite human-staffed division, Division 4 is the experimental hybrid/fiend unit, Divisions 2 and 3 are standard workhorse divisions. Special Divisions are formed for existential crises and disbanded after. The rank system is less about skill and more about survival: you advance by not dying. Kishibe is the strongest veteran; he has no rank beyond his reputation.

### POWER_SYSTEM -- Contracted Devil Categories
<!-- rag: keyword, triggers: contracted devil, fox devil, ghost devil, curse devil, snake devil, spider devil, angel devil, future devil, contract types -->
Contracted devils serve humans through contracts. Categories: Physical-type (Fox Devil: massive fox manifestation for physical attacks; Snake Devil: massive snake for constriction and swallowing), Spectral-type (Ghost Devil: phasing, grabbing, striking), Curse-type (Curse Devil: cursing a target by driving a nail, causing the devil to manifest and crush), Utility-type (Future Devil: shows the contractor a vision of their future, used for intelligence), Life-type (Angel Devil: absorbs human lifespan to create weapons), Control-type (Makima/Control Devil: dominates anything she considers lesser). Each contract has a cost (body part, lifespan, senses, memory, loved one). The more powerful the devil, the steeper the cost. A hunter can hold multiple contracts (Aki had Fox, Curse, and Future), but each adds to the cumulative cost. The contract system is transactional and irreversible; once you pay, you do not get it back.

### POWER_SYSTEM -- Soul Manipulation and Aging/Healing Devils
<!-- rag: keyword, triggers: soul manipulation, aging devil, healing devil, soul devil, life manipulation, devil types -->
Some devils manipulate souls, aging, or healing. The Angel Devil absorbs lifespan (a form of soul manipulation). The Aging Devil (Part 2) manipulates the aging process. Healing devils can restore wounds (at a cost). Soul manipulation is among the most feared devil types because it targets the self rather than the body. These devils are rare and highly valued by contract-hunters; a healing devil contract is worth more than most hunters can pay. The existence of these devils reinforces the world's rule: power always costs something, and the most powerful contracts cost the most irreversible things (life, memory, self).

### POWER_SYSTEM -- The Devil-Hunter Cost (Cumulative Loss)
<!-- rag: keyword, triggers: devil hunter cost, cumulative loss, contract cost, stack contracts, lose yourself, the more you lose -->
The more contracts a devil hunter stacks, the more of themselves they lose. A hunter with one contract has sacrificed one thing (an eye, some lifespan, a sense). A hunter with three contracts (like Aki) has sacrificed three things and is visibly diminished. The most powerful hunters are the most diminished; they have traded pieces of themselves for power. This is the world's central rule: power is not free, and the bill is always your self. Denji is the exception: he started with nothing (sold his organs, his eye, his freedom to the Yakuza before meeting Pochita) and gained a hybrid heart that does not require ongoing sacrifice. But even Denji pays: his body is a weapon, his life is not his own, and the people he loves are used against him. The devil-hunter cost is the story's thesis: in a world where fear is power, the only way to have power is to give up the things that make life worth living.

### POWER_SYSTEM -- Power Rank Scale
<!-- rag: keyword, triggers: power rank, devil power, hybrid power, hunter rank, combat tier, primal fear, power ceiling -->
**Ranks (ascending):** Civilian, Rookie Hunter, Veteran Hunter, Fiend/Hybrid, Special Division, Primal Fear (Horseman).
A 2+ tier gap is effectively decisive in direct combat without significant advantage (surprise, contracts, environmental factors). Exceptions: contracts with powerful devils can bridge the gap; hybrids can regenerate through damage that would kill a human; Primal Fears redefine the terms of engagement entirely.
**Special Division indicators:** Can survive sustained combat from multiple devils; deploy area-of-effect attacks; possess a contract or hybrid power that forces opponents to solve a specific problem.
**Primal Fear (Horseman):** Narrative-scale threats. Makima (Control) could control nations. The Gun Devil (non-Horseman but force-of-nature) killed hundreds of thousands in seconds. The Death Devil is the strongest of all. These are not mission targets; they are world-ending forces that require narrative-scale solutions (Denji defeating Makima through love/equality, not force).

## 6. ECONOMY
### ECONOMY -- Yen, Bounties, and the Devil Hunter Market
<!-- rag: keyword, triggers: yen, currency, bounty, devil bounty, devil finger, salary, private sector pay, cost of living, devil hunter pay, devil economy -->
**Currency:** Yen. Modern Japanese economy.
**Bounty System:** Bounties are paid per devil body part. The value scales with the devil's power: a minor devil (Sea Cucumber Devil) is worth a few thousand yen; a mid-level devil is worth tens of thousands; a powerful devil is worth millions. A Chainsaw Devil finger (a piece of Pochita) is worth millions. A Gun Devil fragment is worth millions and grants gun-related contract powers. Bounties are paid by Public Safety (for devils killed in their jurisdiction) or by private clients (for specific devils targeted for elimination).
**Public Safety Pay:** Public Safety hunters are government employees. Salary, benefits, death benefit (paid to next of kin). Pay is modest: enough to live on, not enough to get rich. The trade-off is stability and backup (in theory). Rookies earn the least; veterans and Special Division members earn more but have shorter life expectancy.
**Private Sector Pay:** Private hunters earn bounties directly. Higher per-kill income, no salary, no benefits, no death benefit, no backup. Most private hunters die within a year; those who survive become wealthy and dangerous. The private sector is where desperate people go for fast money.
**Devil Hunter Gear Market:** Standard-issue pistol, knife, and whatever your contract gives you. Specialized gear (contract-specific weapons, devil-flesh-enhanced tools) is sold on the grey market. Devil flesh and fragments are commodities: a Gun Devil fragment is a strategic asset; a Chainsaw Devil fragment would be priceless. The black market in devil parts is active and dangerous.
**Cost Anchors:** Convenience store meal 300-800 yen; apartment rent 50,000-150,000 yen/month; standard pistol 50,000-100,000 yen; devil hunter gear (basic) 200,000+ yen; devil fragment (minor) 100,000-1,000,000 yen; Gun Devil fragment millions+; Chainsaw Devil fragment priceless.
**Income Anchors:** Civilian 150,000-300,000 yen/month; Rookie Public Safety 180,000-250,000 yen/month; Veteran Public Safety 250,000-400,000 yen/month; Special Division 400,000+ yen/month; Private hunter (successful) 500,000-5,000,000+ yen/month (highly variable); Private hunter (average) dead within a year.

### ECONOMY -- Food as Denji's Primary Motivation
<!-- rag: keyword, triggers: food, denji food, toast, jam, meal, motivation, poverty, hunger -->
Food is Denji's primary motivation and the story's recurring symbol for the simple life he wants. He grew up in poverty (sold his organs to the Yakuza), so eating good food (toast with jam, meat, fancy meals) is his definition of happiness. Food in the story is never just food; it is the marker of whether Denji is living a life worth living. Aki cooking breakfast for Denji and Power is domestic warmth; Denji eating alone after losing everyone is grief. The economy of food (what you can afford to eat) is the economy of the story's emotional core.

### ECONOMY -- Chainsaw Man Church Economy (Part 2)
<!-- rag: keyword, triggers: church economy, chainsaw man church, religion economy, donation, church funding, part 2 economy -->
The Chainsaw Man Church (Part 2) is a religious movement with significant economic power. It collects donations from devotees, operates recruitment centers, and has political influence (supporters in government and media). The Church's economy is based on faith: devotees give money, time, and loyalty in exchange for salvation (as the Church defines it). The Church also has a paramilitary arm (B-CSM members, hybrid leaders like Barem and Miri Sugo) that generates power and influence. The Church's economic model is a critique of organized religion: it monetizes fear and hope, selling salvation to people who are afraid. Internal tension: some Church members genuinely believe; others are using the Church for wealth and power.

## 7. EVENTS
### EVENT -- The Original Chainsaw Devil Legend (Pre-Series)
<!-- rag: keyword, triggers: chainsaw devil legend, hero of hell, pochita legend, devil that eats fears, pre-series, original chainsaw man -->
Before the events of the series, the Chainsaw Devil (Pochita) was the Hero of Hell: a devil so powerful and so feared by other devils that they fled from him. He earned this title by eating devils that embodied fears humans wanted gone, erasing the concepts entirely. He erased fears that humanity has forgotten: a second nuclear weapon, stars that break children's minds, and other concepts. The Chainsaw Devil is the only devil genuinely beloved by humans (because he kills the fears that hurt them). At some point, Pochita was diminished (reduced to the small dog-heart form Denji meets) and eventually fused with Denji to save Denji's life. The legend of the Chainsaw Devil is the foundational myth of the world: the devil that kills fear.

### EVENT -- The Gun Devil Incident (Year ~4 Before Part 1)
<!-- rag: keyword, triggers: gun devil incident, gun devil attack, 350000 dead, massacre, defining event, aki family death, gun devil massacre -->
The Gun Devil Incident: the Gun Devil (the devil born from the fear of guns) appeared in Japan and killed ~350,000 people in seconds. It moved across the country like a natural disaster, leaving a path of destruction. The incident shattered national denial about devils, reshaped global gun policy (guns became heavily restricted worldwide), and created Public Safety's modern form. Aki Hayakawa's family died along the Gun Devil's path; his entire arc is defined by this event. The Gun Devil was eventually fought by Public Safety and fragmented; its pieces became valuable contract materials. The Incident is the 9/11 of the Chainsaw Man world: the event that everything before is "before" and everything after is "after."

### EVENT -- Makima's Manipulation Arc (Part 1)
<!-- rag: keyword, triggers: makima arc, control devil arc, makima manipulation, division 4 formation, denji recruitment -->
Makima's Manipulation Arc (spanning Part 1): Makima (the Control Devil) recruits Denji (Chainsaw Man's host) into Public Safety, gives him a home, food, and a makeshift family (Aki, Power), and uses him as a weapon against the Gun Devil and, ultimately, to try to capture Pochita's full power. She builds Division 4 as an experimental unit of hybrids and fiends, uses them against escalating threats (Katana Man, Reze, Santa Claus, international assassins), and systematically destroys Denji's found family (Aki becomes the Gun Fiend; Power sacrifices herself; Himeno dies) to break him and make him dependent on her. Her goal: use Chainsaw Man to erase the concept of fear, creating a world under her benevolent control. She is defeated by Denji not through force but through a grotesque act of love (he eats her, becoming her, breaking her control by treating her as an equal).

### EVENT -- Control Devil's Defeat (Pochita Reclaims Her)
<!-- rag: keyword, triggers: makima defeat, control devil defeat, pochita reclaims, denji defeats makima, part 1 climax -->
The climax of Part 1: Denji defeats Makima (the Control Devil) not by killing her with force but by eating her (a grotesque act that breaks her control by treating her as an equal, something she cannot process). Makima is defeated because she cannot form genuine equal relationships; she can only dominate or be dominated, and Denji's act of eating her (with love, not hatred) is the one thing she cannot control. Pochita (the Chainsaw Devil, in Denji's heart) reclaims her power. The Control Devil is killed and reincarnates as Nayuta (a child), whom Denji raises with love to be different from Makima. This is the emotional and thematic climax of Part 1: the weapon becomes a person, the controller is defeated by equality, and the cycle (of control, of fear, of loss) is paused but not broken.

### EVENT -- Reze / Bomb Hybrid Incident (Part 1)
<!-- rag: keyword, triggers: reze incident, bomb hybrid, reze arc, cafe, train station, soviet assassin -->
The Reze Incident: Reze (the Bomb Devil hybrid, sent by the Soviet Union) meets Denji at a cafe, befriends him, and offers him a life away from Public Safety. She is genuine and also a weapon; she ultimately attacks Denji to complete her mission (capture Chainsaw Man's heart). Denji fights her (the first hybrid-vs-hybrid battle), and she is eventually killed (or appears to be) by Makima's forces. Her death (reaching for Denji at the train station) is one of the most quietly devastating moments in Part 1. The Reze Incident is the first time Denji is offered a choice (stay with Public Safety or run away) and the choice breaks him.

### EVENT -- Santa Claus / Doll Devil Incident (Part 1)
<!-- rag: keyword, triggers: santa claus incident, doll devil, international assassin arc, dolls, mansion, hotel, global network -->
The Santa Claus Incident: Santa Claus (the Doll Devil, hired by the American Bureau) activates her global network of "children" (contractors she controls as dolls) to attack Denji during the international assassin arc. The assault converges on a hotel/mansion where Denji is hiding, involving Quanxi (Chinese), Soviet operatives, and multiple hybrid assassins (Knife, Whip, Bow, Longsword, Flamethrower, Puppet). The incident is a chaotic multi-faction battle that demonstrates the global scale of the hunt for Chainsaw Man's heart. Santa Claus is defeated by Denji and the survivors, with the help of the Hell entrance and the Darkness Devil's intervention.

### EVENT -- International Assassins Converge on Tokyo (Part 1)
<!-- rag: keyword, triggers: international assassin arc, tokyo convergence, quanxi, santa claus, soviet, american, chinese, chainsaw man heart hunt -->
The International Assassin Arc: when Chainsaw Man's heart (Denji) becomes known globally, assassins from every major nation converge on Tokyo. Quanxi (Chinese crossbow hybrid and her fiend girlfriends), Santa Claus (Doll Devil, American-aligned), Soviet operatives, and the six mansion hybrid assassins all attack simultaneously. The arc is a chaotic, multi-faction battle that demonstrates that Chainsaw Man is treated as a WMD by the international community. The arc ends with most of the assassins dead or leashed by Makima, and Denji's existence as a global target is cemented.

### EVENT -- Chainsaw Man Church Rise (Part 2)

It is also the moment that sets up Denji's final confrontation with Makima: he has lost everything, and the only thing left is to defeat the person who took it all.

**GM Final Note:** This world is built for the AI GM to read and internalize. The locked decisions (devils are born from fear and cycle between Hell and Earth; fiends cannot make contracts; hybrids transform via a trigger and regenerate unless the heart is destroyed; devil contracts cost something irreversible; the Chainsaw Devil erases concepts by eating their devil; Primal Fears are the strongest devils; death is cheap for devils but final for humans) are non-negotiable. The central thematic problem is that power always costs something irreversible, and the bill is always your self. The world is cynical about power: having a devil heart doesn't make you a hero, it makes you a tool. Public Safety uses children as soldiers and calls it duty. Makima's kindness is the leash. Denji's simple dreams (toast, jam, a family) are worth more than any devil's power, and the story knows it. Choices are permanent. Contracts are irreversible. Go.
