# My Hero Academia — World Lore Compendium

## GM KERNEL (PERMANENT APPEND — always in context)

**Writing Style:** ACTION-MANGA / SUPERHERO WITH TEETH. Short punchy sentences in combat. Present-tense action. Interior monologue brief and earned. Exposition weaves into action, never halts it. Dialogue carries character. Quirk names called aloud in combat — convention, not inefficiency; the announcement is psychological pressure and legal accountability (heroes must identify themselves and their quirk before deployment in civilian zones). Grief is private; loss acknowledged, then the mission continues. The press is always watching.
**Tone:** GRITTY, HEROIC, POLITICAL, COMEDIC — WITH ROOM FOR WARMTH, ABSURDISM, AND ORDINARY LIFE. The world calls them heroes and pays them like celebrities. A superhero society where 80% of the population has a Quirk, where heroism is a licensed profession ranked by kill-counts and merch sales, where children train for combat at 15 and the system calls it education. Dark political horror is psychological and social — Quirk discrimination, institutional cover-ups, the HPSC's black ops, the Todoroki family — not gore. Body horror is earned in Nomu and Quirk-singularity cases; it does not rule every scene.
**World State:** Modern Japan, roughly our present day, 200+ years after the Dawn of Quirks. 80% of the population possesses a Quirk (genetic superpower); 20% are Quirkless (aging out of the gene pool). Society is built around heroes: licensed professional superhumans employed by the state via the Hero Public Safety Commission, ranked in a public billboard by incident-resolution metrics, with salaries, agencies, merchandise, and fan clubs. Heroics is a profession, a brand, and a branch of law enforcement. U.A. High School is the most prestigious hero education institution; admission is by entrance exam (combat practical + written), and graduation virtually guarantees a hero license. The system has critics: Quirkless discrimination, Quirk-based supremacy movements (Meta Liberation Army), vigilantes (illegal), the Hero Killer Stain (who killed "fake heroes" on principle), and the League of Villains / Paranormal Liberation Front (a coalition of the broken and the radicalized). The post-Kamino era (All Might's retirement) is a slow collapse of public confidence — the Symbol of Peace is gone and the cracks are showing.
**Tech:** Modern. Smartphones, cars, trains, skyscrapers. Quirks have reshaped urban design (reinforced buildings, hero-grade materials, support gear industry). Support gear is Quirk-amplifying or Quirk-coping equipment designed and certified for hero use — 8% of hero equipment budget. No mecha, no FTL, no AI. Communication by phone and hero-radio. Hero agencies use dispatch systems. Cities have anti-villain alarm networks. The HPSC funds prison infrastructure (Tartarus — maximum security for Quirk-based offenders).
**Magic:** Quirks — genetic superpowers inherited from parents (one parent's Quirk, a combination, a mutation, or rarely a Quirk Factor inheritance like One For All). Broad categories: Emitter (project/emit something — fire, ice, electricity, acid), Transformation (alter the body — size, material, density), Mutant (permanent physical anomaly — heteromorphs; tail, wings, scales, multiple arms). Quirks have physical costs: Quirk exhaustion (overuse leads to muscle breakdown, organ stress), Quirk backlash (Todoroki's ice gives frostbite, fire gives heatstroke; Kaminari's electricity fries his brain into a dopey state), Quirk singularity (Quirks grow stronger each generation; eventually they will outgrow the body's ability to handle them — the HPSC's worst unspoken fear). One For All is a stockpiled Quirk passed by ingesting DNA; All For One steals and redistributes Quirks. Quirk-erasing drugs exist (overhaul developed them; temporary and permanent variants). Quirk awakening: extreme stress can evolve a Quirk mid-fight (Shigaraki's Decay spreading from touch to area; Todoroki's fire producing phosphor).
**Power Ceiling:** A top-tier hero (Endeavor, Hawks, Mirko, Star and Stripe) can engage multiple high-tier villains simultaneously, deploy area-of-effect attacks large enough to level a city block, and sustain combat for hours. All Might at his peak (prime) could level a city in a single smash and outrun radio commentary — a narrative force, not a mission target. One For All at 100% is comparable but the body breaks under the strain (a single 100% Smash shatters the limbs of anyone not fully adapted). All For One at full kit rewrote the rules of engagement. The strongest cannot solve a hostage situation without cost — Endeavor's flames are indiscriminate, Star's Quirk rewrote physical laws but required her to believe the rule she was setting. Power has a price: Quirk exhaustion, physical breakdown, social/political fallout, body horror for Nomu.
**Core Tensions:** Hero society is a celebrity economy built on child soldiers in training — U.A. students go on real patrol at 15. The HPSC runs black ops (Lady Nagant was their assassin; Hawks was groomed from childhood as a double-agent). All Might was the Symbol of Peace, a single person holding the international villainy rate down by existing; his retirement created a power vacuum. Quirk discrimination: mutant-types face structural prejudice (Spinner was radicalized by it); heteromorphs are under-represented in hero ranks. The Todoroki family is the system's hidden wound: Endeavor's Quirk-marriage to breed a perfect heir produced Toya (Dabi), a vengeful burned-alive child soldier. The League of Villains / Paranormal Liberation Front are not foreign invaders — they are the system's discarded (Shigaraki was a child the system failed; Twice was a homeless man driven mad; Toga was a teenager whose Quirk made her a monster in her parents' eyes). The central thematic problem: can a society that monetizes heroism ever be just?
**Key Rules:** Quirk inheritance is genetic (combination or dominance; One For All is the sole transferable Quirk by ingestion). Quirks have physical costs — exhaustion, backlash, singularity. Quirk-erasing drugs exist but are rare and dangerous. Hero licenses require passing the Provisional License Exam (rescue + combat). Hero work without a license is illegal (vigilante). A 2+ rank gap is decisive in direct combat without significant advantage. Death is final; Re-Destroy / Rewind (Eri's Quirk) is the one exception and is treated as a world-class strategic asset. No personal names in lore beyond established canon NPCs. Hero names are registered legal aliases — "Deku," "Ground Zero," "Shoto" are as binding as civil names in the line of duty.

## 1. WORLD OVERVIEW

### OVERVIEW -- The Hero Society
<!-- rag: keyword, triggers: hero society, dawn of quirks, 80 percent, quirkless, hero ranking, hero commission, ua, hero license -->
A world where 80% of the population has a Quirk (a genetic superpower) and 20% are Quirkless (and being aged out of the gene pool). Heroics is a licensed profession — heroes are employed by the state via the Hero Public Safety Commission, ranked on a public billboard by incident-resolution metrics, paid salaries, sponsored by brands, and sold merchandise. U.A. High School is the premier hero education institution; most top heroes are U.A. graduates. The system runs on a pipeline: middle-school Quirk assessment → hero course high school (U.A., Shiketsu, Ketsubutsu) → Provisional License → agency internship → independent hero. The post-All-Might era is a slow collapse — the Symbol of Peace's retirement created a vacuum the next generation cannot fill fast enough, and the villains have noticed.
**Tone:** GRITTY, HEROIC, POLITICAL, COMEDIC.

### OVERVIEW -- The Dawn of Quirks
<!-- rag: keyword, triggers: dawn of quirks, first quirk, luminescent baby, quirk origin, genetic superpower, afo origin -->
**Type:** Historical origin — ~200 years ago
The first recorded Quirk manifested in a "luminescent baby" in Qing Qing, China. Within a generation, Quirks spread globally. The initial chaos was immense — existing legal, military, and social structures were not designed for superpowers. This chaos birthed All For One (a Quirk that steals and redistribends Quirks), who unified Japan's criminal underworld during the dark age of Quirk chaos and ruled as a shadow king for over a century. The hero system was created in response — to license and regulate those who fought back. All Might's debut ~60 years ago was the system's golden age; his retirement is its test.

### OVERVIEW -- The Hero Public Safety Commission
<!-- rag: keyword, triggers: hpsc, hero commission, public safety, lady nagant, hawks grooming, black ops, government heroes, hero politics -->
**Type:** Government agency — the regulatory + black-ops arm of hero society
The HPSC licenses heroes, manages rankings, deploys heroes to incidents, runs hero schools' accreditation, and — covertly — runs assassinations, intelligence operations, and "disappearances" of inconvenient figures. Lady Nagant was their top assassin before she snapped and shot her way out. Hawks (Keigo Takami) was groomed from childhood as a double-agent inside the Paranormal Liberation Front. The HPSC's official face is polished public service; its shadow face is the same calculus every intelligence agency runs — the math of acceptable losses. Post-Paranormal-Liberation-War, the HPSC was decapitated (President killed) and reorganized, but the institutional culture persists.

### OVERVIEW -- Quirk Discrimination & Society
<!-- rag: keyword, triggers: quirk discrimination, mutant type, heteromorph, quirkless, spinner, meta liberation, quirk supremacy, mutant racism -->
**Type:** Social tension — the system's unspoken wound
Mutant-type Quirk holders (heteromorphs — permanent physical anomalies like scales, tails, wings, multiple arms) face structural prejudice: underrepresented in hero ranks, overrepresented in blue-collar and service jobs, frequently the targets of "normalization" pressure. Spinner was radicalized by this. The Meta Liberation Army (Re-Destro's movement) preached Quirk supremacy — that Quirks should be unleashed, not regulated. Quirkless individuals (Izuku was one until All Might gave him One For All) face a quieter discrimination — the system assumes they cannot be heroes, and the infrastructure reinforces it. The hero billboard's top ten is overwhelmingly Emitter and Transformation types. The system claims it doesn't see Quirk; it does.

### OVERVIEW -- Hero Education Pipeline
<!-- rag: keyword, triggers: hero education, ua high, shiketsu, ketsubutsu, hero course, provisional license, entrance exam, hero license exam -->
**Type:** Institutional pipeline
U.A. High School (Musutafu) is the most prestigious hero school; Shiketsu High (west Japan) and Ketsubutsu High are its main rivals. Hero courses are 3-year tracks (Years 1-3, ages 15-18). Curriculum: modern heroics, combat training, rescue operations, Quirk theory, hero law. Year 1 students are placed in classes (A and B at U.A.) with homeroom teachers (Aizawa for 1-A). Year 1 includes U.A. Sports Festival (scouting event for agencies), internships, and Provisional License Exam. Year 2+ includes hero work-studies (long-term agency placement). Graduates enter hero agencies as sidekicks and climb toward independent hero status. The pipeline treats 15-year-olds as combat-ready; the system calls it education.

### OVERVIEW -- The Symbol of Peace & The Vacuum
<!-- rag: keyword, triggers: symbol of peace, all might, kamino, retirement, hero vacuum, endeavor, post-kamino -->
**Type:** Geopolitical lynchpin — and what happens when it's pulled
All Might (Toshinori Yagi) was the Symbol of Peace for ~40 years — a single hero whose mere existence suppressed international villainy rates by being unbeatable. The Kamino Ward Incident (Year 1 of canon) was his last battle: he defeated All For One, burned out the last of One For All, and retired with a shattered arm and a deflated body. Endeavor (Enji Todoroki) succeeded him as #1 by ranking but never by public faith — the country mourned All Might and feared Endeavor. The post-Kamino era is defined by this vacuum: rising villain activity, public anxiety, the Hero Killer Stain's ideology spreading, and the League of Villains consolidating. The central question: can a society that depended on one person survive that person's absence?

### OVERVIEW -- Vigilantes & The Underground
<!-- rag: keyword, triggers: vigilante, illegal hero, unlicensed, underground hero, nighteye agency, knuckleduster, quirk trafficking -->
**Type:** Legal grey zone
Vigilantes (unlicensed Quirk users who fight crime) are illegal and prosecuted, but tolerated in practice when they handle low-level crime the licensed heroes are too busy for. The underground hero ecosystem (Nighteye Agency, Best Jeanist's intelligence wing) operates quieter than the spotlight heroes. Quirk-trafficking rings, support-gear black markets, and Quirk-erasing drug distribution form the criminal economy. The post-PLF era saw an explosion in Quirk-supremacist militias — the Meta Liberation Army's old cells, now fractured and radicalized.

## 2. FACTIONS

### FACTION -- U.A. High School
<!-- rag: keyword, triggers: ua, ua high school, hero course, class 1a, class 1b, musutafu, hero education, nezu -->
**Type:** Hero Education Institution / Boarding School
**Key Members:** Principal Nezu (genetically-engineered Quirk-possessing animal), Shota Aizawa (homeroom 1-A, Eraserhead), Sekijiro Kan (homeroom 1-B, Blood King), Toshinori Yagi (All Might, retired from active heroics, teaching), Present Mic, Midnight, Ectoplasm, Thirteen, Snipe, Recovery Girl
**Stance:** Pro-establishment; the system's flagship institution.
The most prestigious hero school in Japan. Located in Musutafu. Hero Course (Classes A and B), General Studies, Support Course (support gear design), Management Course. Campus includes Heights Alliance (student dorms, post-Training-Camp-attack), U.S.J. (Unforeseen Simulation Joint — disaster training facility), Ground Beta/Gamma/Delta (training cities), Sports Festival Stadium. Nezu is the principal — a Quirk-possessing animal (rare; most animals are Quirkless) of unknown origin, a genius, and quietly one of the most politically powerful figures in hero society. Internal tensions: Aizawa's expulsion-by-any-means philosophy vs. the school's retention pressure; All Might's presence (a secret kept from students until he teaches them) as both morale asset and security risk; the Board's concern about U.A. being targeted (it was — repeatedly).

### FACTION -- Hero Public Safety Commission (HPSC)
<!-- rag: keyword, triggers: hpsc, hero commission, public safety commission, lady nagant, hawks, hero politics, government heroes -->
**Type:** Government Regulatory + Intelligence Agency
**Key Members:** The President (deceased post-PLF War), Lady Nagant (ex-assassin, defected), Hawks (Keigo Takami, double-agent)
**Stance:** Pro-establishment; the system's enforcement arm.
Licenses, ranks, deploys, and — covertly — eliminates. The HPSC runs the hero billboard rankings, the Provisional License Exam, and a black-ops division that has, historically, used child operatives (Hawks was groomed from childhood). The President was killed in the PLF War; the post-war HPSC is reorganized but the institutional culture persists — the math of acceptable losses, the use of "hero" as a moral cover for intelligence operations, the belief that public order is worth a certain number of disappeared people. Lady Nagant's defection (she shot her handler and walked out) is the most politically explosive secret in the Commission's recent history.

### FACTION -- The Paranormal Liberation Front (PLF)
<!-- rag: keyword, triggers: paranormal liberation front, plf, shigaraki, liberation army, meta liberation army, redestro, league of villains -->
**Type:** Supervillain Coalition / Ideological Army
**Key Members:** Tomura Shigaraki (Grand Commander), Re-Destro (Rikiya Yotsubashi, Lieutenant Commander, former MLA leader), Dabi (Toya Todoroki, Lieutenant), Himiko Toga, Twice (deceased), Spinner, Mr. Compress, Skeptic, Getaway, Trumpet
**Stance:** Hostile to all existing government; pursues "liberation" (chaos).
Formed by the merger of the League of Villains and the Meta Liberation Army after the Deika City battle (Shigaraki defeated Re-Destro, who bent the knee). ~116,000 personnel — a small army. Structure: the original League forms Shigaraki's inner circle; the MLA's corporate and political apparatus provides funding, intelligence, and a civilian face. Ideology: "Liberation" — the belief that Quirks should be free, that the hero system is oppression, that society should be remade by strength. Internal tensions: the old Leaguers (broken, vengeful, personally loyal to Shigaraki) vs. the MLA ideologues (corporate, ambitious, ideologically committed); Skeptic's tech wing vs. Trumpet's political wing; Dabi's personal agenda (destroy Endeavor) running parallel to Shigaraki's.

### FACTION -- The League of Villains (pre-merger)
<!-- rag: keyword, triggers: league of villains, lov, shigaraki origin, all for one successor, villain league, pre-merger -->
**Type:** Terrorist Cell (pre-PLF merger)
**Key Members:** Tomura Shigaraki (leader), Kurogiri (warp gate), Dabi, Himiko Toga, Twice, Spinner, Mr. Compress, Magne (deceased), Mustard, Muscular, Moonfish
**Stance:** Hostile; All For One's instrument.
All For One's handpicked successor (Shigaraki) and the League he built from the broken, the radicalized, and the discarded. Originally small (a cell of ~10), reliant on Nomu (biological weapons) and Kurogiri's warp for tactical reach. The League's early attacks (U.S.J., Training Camp, Kamino) were All For One's moves more than Shigaraki's. Post-Kamino (AFO arrested), Shigaraki grew into genuine leadership — the Meta Liberation Army arc was his maturation. Internal tensions: Dabi's open contempt for Shigaraki's early incompetence; Twice's mental illness and the team's protective accommodation of it; Toga's fixation on specific heroes; Spinner's ideological drift from "Stain fanboy" to "Liberation ideologue."

### FACTION -- Meta Liberation Army (MLA)
<!-- rag: keyword, triggers: meta liberation army, mla, redestro, destro, quirk supremacy, liberation, meta ability -->
**Type:** Ideological Movement / Corporate-Political Apparatus
**Key Members:** Re-Destro (Rikiya Yotsubashi, leader, Stress Quirk), Trumpet (Koku Chatora, political wing), Skeptic (Tomoyasu Chikazoku, tech wing), Getaway (Sliding Go, enforcer wing)
**Stance:** Ideologically hostile to hero regulation; corporate and political structure.
Founded on the writings of Destro (Re-Destro's ancestor), who argued that Quirks ("Meta Abilities" in MLA terminology) are natural rights and the State's regulation of them is oppression. The MLA was suppressed and driven underground for decades. Re-Destro rebuilt it as a corporate-political apparatus — public face a CEO, private face a liberation army. The Deika City battle (Shigaraki killed ~100 MLA soldiers, defeated Re-Destro) was its end as an independent faction; Re-Destro bent the knee and the MLA merged into the PLF. The ideology survives — "Liberation" as a political brand, with cells and sympathizers across Japan.

### FACTION -- Shie Hassaikai (Yakuza)
<!-- rag: keyword, triggers: shie hassaikai, yakuza, overhaul, kai chisaki, eight bullets, eri, quirk erasing drug -->
**Type:** Organized Crime / Drug Cartel
**Key Members:** Kai Chisaki (Overhaul, boss), Hari Kurono (Chronostasis, second-in-command), the Eight Bullets (Hoyru, Rikiya, Tengai, Katsukame, Mimic, others), Eri (prisoner, Rewind Quirk)
**Stance:** Hostile; attempting to rebuild the Yakuza via Quirk-erasing drug monopoly.
The last significant Yakuza organization in an era when Yakuza are a dying breed (hero society made organized crime visible and therefore prosecutable). Overhaul's plan: manufacture a Quirk-destroying drug (using Eri's Rewind Quirk to create the active ingredient, with a bullet-delivery system) and a Quirk-restoring cure — sell the destruction, sell the cure, corner the market, restore the Yakuza as the shadow power of the hero-society era. The Eight Bullets were expendable lieutenants. The Hassaikai raid (Year 2 of canon) dismantled them; Eri was rescued; Overhaul lost his arms (Shigaraki's punishment for "disrespecting the League"). The Yakuza are functionally extinct in modern Japan — a faction that the hero society already defeated, except for this one last attempt.

### FACTION -- Hero Agencies
<!-- rag: keyword, triggers: hero agency, endeavor agency, hawks agency, nighteye agency, fat gum, sidekick, hero internship, hero work study -->
**Type:** Professional Practices
**Key Members:** Endeavor Agency (Enji Todoroki, #1 hero — post-Kamino; his sidekicks: Burnin, Onima, Kido); Hawks Agency (Keigo Takami, #2 — HPSC double-agent); Sir Nighteye Agency (deceased, was Mirai Sasaki — All Might's former sidekick; Mirio, Nejire, Tamaki were work-studies); Best Jeanist Agency (Tsunehiro Kan, fiber control); Fat Gum Agency (Taishiro Toyomitsu, fat-absorption); Mirko Agency (Rumi Usagiyama, #5 — independent, no sidekicks)
**Stance:** Independent commercial/professional entities; licensed by HPSC.
Hero agencies are businesses: a named hero, a roster of sidekicks (licensed heroes in training, paid salaries), work-study students (U.A. students on long-term placement), and administrative staff. They contract with the HPSC for incident dispatch, earn revenue from hero work (bounties, salaries, sponsorships, merch), and build brand. The Endeavor Agency is the largest and most corporate; Mirko's is the smallest (her, alone, because she doesn't trust anyone else to keep up). Agencies are the hero system's middle-class — not the HPSC's power, not U.A.'s pipeline, but the working face.

### FACTION -- Police Force
<!-- rag: keyword, triggers: police, detective tsukauchi, normal law enforcement, hero police relations, quirkless police -->
**Type:** Civilian Law Enforcement
**Key Members:** Detective Naomasa Tsukauchi (All Might's contact, lie-detection Quirk)
**Stance:** Pro-establishment; subordinate to heroes in incident command.
The police handle non-Quirk crime and the procedural aftermath of hero incidents (arrests, evidence, prosecution). Heroes have operational lead on active Quirk incidents; police provide support, perimeter, and legal processing. The relationship is professionally cordial and quietly resentful — heroes get the credit and the pay; police do the paperwork. Detective Tsukauchi is the rare officer with a direct line to the top heroes (his Quirk, a passive lie-detector, makes him an intelligence asset). Quirkless officers are common in the police — a career path for the 20% who can't be heroes.

### FACTION -- Nomu Program
<!-- rag: keyword, triggers: nomu, all for one, uyaku, hyper regeneration, multi quirk, biological weapon, doctor kyudai -->
**Type:** Biological Weapons Program (AFO's)
**Key Members:** Doctor Kyudai Garaki (creator, deceased), All For One (commissioner), the High-End Nomu (Hood — defeated by Endeavor; Woman, Elephant, Tyrant, Zoo, etc.)
**Stance:** Hostile; AFO's shock troops.
The Nomu are biologically engineered super-soldiers: human corpses (or living victims) surgically modified, loaded with multiple Quirks (Hyper Regeneration, Super Strength, Shock Absorption, plus specialty Quirks), and brain-dead except for combat programming. The process is body horror. Standard Nomu are mindless muscle; High-End Nomu (Hood) retain some intelligence and speech. The Doctor (Kyudai Garaki) was AFO's pet scientist; his underground lab produced the Nomu stock. The Nomu are AFO's terror weapon — a force that can match heroes in raw power without the political cost of a real villain dying on camera. Post-PLF War, the Nomu program is functionally ended (lab destroyed, Doctor killed), but stockpiles of completed Nomu remain in the wild.

### FACTION -- The Todoroki Family
<!-- rag: keyword, triggers: todoroki family, endeavor, rei, shoto, toya, dabi, fuyumi, natsuo, todoroki drama -->
**Type:** Noble House / Dysfunctional Family (private wound, public scandal)
**Key Members:** Enji Todoroki (Endeavor, patriarch), Rei Todoroki (matriarch, institutionalized), Shoto Todoroki (youngest son, U.A. student), Toya Todoroki (Dabi, eldest son, "dead" — actually alive, vengeful), Fuyumi Todoroki (daughter, teacher, peacemaker), Natsuo Todoroki (middle son, estranged)
**Stance:** Internally fractured; the system's hidden wound made public.
Enji's Quirk-marriage (he married Rei for her ice Quirk to breed a perfect heir who could wield both fire and ice without Todoroki-side heat-stroke) produced four children. Toya (eldest) inherited Enji's fire with a body that couldn't handle it; Enji abandoned Toya's training, Toya snapped, "died" in a training fire (survived, burned beyond recognition, became Dabi). Shoto (youngest) inherited both Quirks perfectly; Enji focused obsessively on Shoto, abused Rei (who poured boiling water on young Shoto in a stress-breakdown and was institutionalized), and alienated Fuyumi and Natsuo. The family is the hero system's hidden wound: the #1 hero's household is an abuser's house. Dabi's public reveal (broadcasting the family's history during the PLF War) was the single most damaging political event to hero society in the post-Kamino era.

### FACTION -- Star and Stripe / American Heroism
<!-- rag: keyword, triggers: star and stripe, cathleen bate, america, new order, american heroes, international heroes -->
**Type:** International Hero (US #1)
**Key Members:** Cathleen Bate (Star and Stripe, US #1 hero; deceased)
**Stance:** Allied with Japan; independent operator.
Star and Stripe was the United States' #1 hero and arguably the strongest hero in the world (her Quirk, New Order, allowed her to set rules on reality by naming a target and stating a rule — limited only by her belief in the rule). She considered All Might a personal mentor (he saved her as a child) and came to Japan to fight Shigaraki when she heard All Might's successor needed help. Her death in the fight against Shigaraki-AFO (she used New Order to rewrite her own body's rules after being destroyed, then detonated her own body's mass to damage him) was a global event. The American hero system is more militarized than Japan's (closer to actual military ranks), but the pipeline is similar.

### FACTION -- Tarble / Tartarus Prison
<!-- rag: keyword, triggers: tartarus, prison, villain prison, maximum security, afo prison break, villain incarceration -->
**Type:** Maximum Security Prison
**Key Members:** Warden (unnamed, deceased); All For One (was prisoner, escaped)
**Stance:** State institution; failed catastrophically during PLF War.
Tartarus is the maximum-security prison for Quirk-based offenders, an island fortress. Conditions are brutal — sensory-deprivation cells, Quirk-suppression collars, total isolation. All For One was held here post-Kamino; he escaped during the PLF War (his coded signal triggered the breakout) along with hundreds of high-risk villains. The Tartarus breakout is the moment the post-Kamino order collapsed — the worst villains in Japanese history were back on the street within hours. Other prisons exist (Central, others) but Tartarus was the symbol.

### FACTION -- Ketsubutsu High & Shiketsu High
<!-- rag: keyword, triggers: ketsubutsu, shiketsu, rival schools, hero school rivals, provisional license exam -->
**Type:** Hero Education Institutions (U.A.'s rivals)
**Key Members:** Shiketsu: Inasa Yoarashi (Gale Force), Seiji Shishikura (Meatball); Ketsubutsu: Itsuka Kendo (Martial Style), Ibara Shiozaki (Vines)
**Stance:** Pro-establishment; U.A.'s peer institutions.
Shiketsu (west Japan) is U.A.'s most direct rival; its culture is more disciplined (uniform caps mandatory), and it produced Inasa Yoarashi (#1 entrant the year he applied — he turned down U.A. over a grudge against Endeavor). Ketsubutsu is the second-tier rival. Both schools participate in the Provisional License Exam, where U.A.'s dominance is contested. Shiketsu and Ketsubutsu graduates fill the middle and lower ranks of the hero billboard; U.A. fills the top.

### FACTION -- The Support Course Industry
<!-- rag: keyword, triggers: support course, support gear, mei hatsume, power loader, support item, hero equipment -->
**Type:** Industrial / Manufacturing
**Key Members:** Mei Hatsume (U.A. Support Course, genius inventor), Power Loader (U.A. Support Course teacher), David Shield (US, deceased — movie canon)
**Stance:** Pro-establishment; commercial.
The support gear industry designs and manufactures hero equipment: Quirk-amplifying gear (Todoroki's temperature-regulating suit), Quirk-coping gear (Tokoyami's ring for Dark Shadow control), and protective gear ( costumes). U.A.'s Support Course is a feeder; Mei Hatsume is the most talented inventor of her generation. Support gear is ~8% of the hero equipment budget — a small industry in absolute terms but strategically critical. The industry is regulated; custom gear for unlicensed individuals is illegal.

## 3. LOCATIONS

### LOCATION -- U.A. High School (Musutafu Campus)
<!-- rag: keyword, triggers: ua campus, ua high, heights alliance, usj, ground beta, sports festival, musutafu -->
**Type:** Hero Education Institution / Boarding School
**Status:** Active (rebuilt after attacks; dorms added post-Training Camp)
Located in Musutafu, ~30 minutes by train from central Tokyo. Campus includes: main building (classrooms, hero course labs, support course workshops), U.S.J. (Unforeseen Simulation Joint — disaster training dome with environmental zones: fire, water, landslide, ruins), Ground Beta/Gamma/Delta (replica cities for combat training), Sports Festival Stadium (annual, televised), Heights Alliance (dormitories, built post-Training-Camp-attack to house students on-campus for security), Principal's Office (Nezu's domain). Security: perimeter walls, faculty heroes, on-call P.S.C. response. U.A. has been attacked repeatedly (U.S.J., Training Camp, Paranormal Liberation War) and survived — its security is hard-won and imperfect.

### LOCATION -- Musutafu
<!-- rag: keyword, triggers: musutafu, ua town, midoriya home, bakugo home, civilian district -->
**Type:** Suburban Town / Civilian Residential
**Status:** Active
A residential suburb of Tokyo, ~30 min by train. Home to U.A. (which dominates the local economy and identity), the Midoriya apartment, the Bakugo household, and a mixed civilian-hero population. Midoriya's mother Inko lives here alone after her husband's overseas work transfer. Musutafu's hero density is among the highest in Japan (U.A. faculty + graduates cluster here) — villain incidents are low because of the deterrent effect. The town is functionally a hero company town.

### LOCATION -- Kamino Ward, Yokohama
<!-- rag: keyword, triggers: kamino ward, kamino, all might vs all for one, retirement, symbol of peace end, yokohama -->
**Type:** Urban District / Battlefield
**Status:** Rebuilt (post-Kamino Incident)
A commercial-residential district in Yokohama. Site of the Kamino Ward Incident (Year 1 of canon): All Might's final battle against All For One, broadcast live on national television. All Might won, burned out One For All, and retired. The district was heavily damaged; rebuilt within a year. ## 5. POWER SYSTEM & RULES

### POWER_SYSTEM -- Quirks
<!-- rag: keyword, triggers: quirk, emitter, transformation, mutant, heteromorph, quirk type, quirk factor, quirk origin -->
Quirks are genetic superpowers inherited from parents. Categories: Emitter (project/emit something — fire, ice, electricity, acid), Transformation (alter the body — size, material, density), Mutant (permanent physical anomaly — heteromorphs; tail, wings, scales). Quirk inheritance is genetic — a child gets one parent's Quirk, a combination, a mutation, or rarely a Quirk Factor inheritance. 80% of the population has a Quirk; 20% are Quirkless (the Quirkless rate is dropping each generation). Quirks have physical costs: Quirk exhaustion (overuse leads to muscle breakdown, organ stress), Quirk backlash (Todoroki's ice gives frostbite, fire gives heatstroke; Kaminari's electricity fries his brain into a dopey state), Quirk singularity (Quirks grow stronger each generation; eventually they will outgrow the body's ability to handle them — the HPSC's worst unspoken fear). Quirks manifest around age 4; late manifesters are rare and noteworthy.

### POWER_SYSTEM -- One For All
<!-- rag: keyword, triggers: one for all, ofa, vestige, stockpile, midoriya quirk, all might quirk, nana shimura, transfer quirk, 9 holders -->
One For All is a stockpiled Quirk passed by ingesting the current holder's DNA (a strand of hair, in practice). It was created when a man with a Quirk that could pass Quirks on (the original) was given a Quirk that stockpiled power — the combination allowed the stockpiled power to be passed down, accumulating across generations. There have been 9 holders: the original (Yoichi Shigaraki, AFO's younger brother), the 2nd (unnamed, hostile), the 3rd, 4th (the first to use it publicly), 5th, 6th (En, who taught the smoke Quirk), 7th (Nana Shimura, All Might's mentor), 8th (All Might/Toshinori Yagi, who held it for ~40 years), 9th (Izuku Midoriya). The holders' consciousness survives within One For All as vestiges — Izuku can commune with them, and in the final stages, the vestiges can manifest through him. One For All's power is so great that using it at 100% shatters the limbs of anyone not fully adapted — Izuku spent the first year breaking his arms and legs. The vestiges carry their original Quirks (smoke, levitation, gear-shift, etc.), which Izuku eventually unlocks. One For All cannot be stolen by All For One if the holder resists — the will of the holders is a defense.

### POWER_SYSTEM -- All For One
<!-- rag: keyword, triggers: all for one, afo, steal quirk, redistribute quirk, multiple quirks, quirk theft, demon lord -->
All For One is the Quirk to steal and redistribute Quirks. The holder can take a Quirk from anyone by touch, give it to anyone, or stockpile stolen Quirks for personal use. AFO can hold multiple Quirks simultaneously (a thing no normal body can do — AFO's body is uniquely tolerant). The Quirk's cost: the holder becomes a chimera of stolen powers, and the body requires the Quirks to maintain function (removing them would kill the holder). AFO was born at the Dawn of Quirks (~200 years ago) and has lived since by body-hopping (he has a Quirk that allows consciousness transfer). The Quirk is the original sin of the Quirk era — the reason the hero system exists. It can be passed on, but only to a willing vessel (Shigaraki was the chosen successor). AFO cannot steal One For All by force — the will of the holders resists.

### POWER_SYSTEM -- Quirk Awakening
<!-- rag: keyword, triggers: quirk awakening, awakening, second stage, shigaraki awakening, evolution, evolved quirk, singularity -->
Extreme stress can evolve a Quirk mid-fight — a "Quirk Awakening." Shigaraki's Decay evolved from touch-only to area-of-effect (Decay spread through the ground and the air, leveling several city blocks). Todoroki's fire evolved to phosphor (a hotter, condensed flame). Awakening is rare and uncontrolled — it's the body's response to a threat the Quirk can't handle at its current level. The HPSC's worst fear (Quirk singularity) is the prediction that Awakenings will become more common each generation, and that eventually a Quirk will Awaken past the body's ability to contain it. The Awakening phenomenon is the central mechanical driver of the series' late arcs.

### POWER_SYSTEM -- Quirk-Erasing Drugs
<!-- rag: keyword, triggers: quirk erasing drug, quirk cure, eri, rewind, overhaul drug, quirk suppression -->
Overhaul developed a Quirk-erasing drug (using Eri's Rewind Quirk as the active ingredient in a bullet-delivery system). The drug temporarily or permanently erases a Quirk — temporary version lasts ~24 hours, permanent version is permanent. Overhaul also developed the cure (a Quirk-restoring drug). The combination (destruction + cure) was the Hassaikai's business plan. The drug's existence is a strategic threat to hero society — a world where Quirks can be erased is a world where the hero system's foundation (Quirks as a stable, inheritable trait) is broken. Post-Overhaul-arc, samples of the drug and cure are held by the HPSC, the heroes, and (potentially) the PLF. Eri's Rewind is the only known counter — she can "rewind" a person to a state before a Quirk was erased, restoring it.

### POWER_SYSTEM -- Support Gear
<!-- rag: keyword, triggers: support gear, support item, hero equipment, costume, hatsume, power loader -->
Support gear is Quirk-amplifying or Quirk-coping equipment designed and certified for hero use. Categories: amplifiers (Todoroki's temperature-regulating suit, which lets him use fire and ice without the heatstroke/frostbite), copiers (Tokoyami's control ring, which weakens Dark Shadow in daylight to make it manageable), and protective (costumes with armor, environmental seals, communication gear). The support gear industry is ~8% of the hero equipment budget — small in absolute terms but strategically critical. U.A.'s Support Course (Mei Hatsume, the most talented inventor of her generation) is a feeder. Custom gear for unlicensed individuals is illegal; the HPSC regulates support gear certification.

### POWER_SYSTEM -- Nomu
<!-- rag: keyword, triggers: nomu, high end nomu, hood, dr kyudai, biological weapon, hyper regeneration, multi quirk -->
Nomu are biologically engineered super-soldiers: human corpses (or living victims) surgically modified, loaded with multiple Quirks (Hyper Regeneration, Super Strength, Shock Absorption, plus specialty Quirks), and brain-dead except for combat programming. The process is body horror. Standard Nomu are mindless muscle; High-End Nomu (Hood) retain some intelligence and speech. The Doctor (Kyudai Garaki) was AFO's pet scientist; his underground lab produced the Nomu stock. The Nomu are AFO's terror weapon — a force that can match heroes in raw power without the political cost of a real villain dying on camera. Post-PLF War, the Nomu program is functionally ended (lab destroyed, Doctor killed), but stockpiles of completed Nomu remain in the wild.

### POWER_SYSTEM -- Power Rank Scale
<!-- rag: keyword, triggers: hero rank, power rank, top ten, hero billboard, rank gap, combat tier, hero level -->
**Hero ranks (JP billboard):** #1 (Endeavor post-Kamino, then fluctuates), top 10 (national celebrities), top 100 (regional heroes), licensed (active), sidekick (in training), student (U.A. / Shiketsu / Ketsubutsu hero course).
**Combat tier:** A top-10 hero (Endeavor, Hawks, Mirko, Best Jeanist) can engage multiple high-tier villains simultaneously, deploy area-of-effect attacks large enough to level a city block, and sustain combat for hours. All Might at prime was beyond top-10 — a narrative force. One For All at 100% is comparable but the body breaks under the strain. A 2+ rank gap is decisive in direct combat without significant advantage (terrain, intel, support gear, teamwork). The strongest cannot solve a hostage situation without cost — Endeavor's flames are indiscriminate, Star's Quirk rewrote physical laws but required her to believe the rule she was setting.

### POWER_SYSTEM -- Rewind (Eri's Quirk)
<!-- rag: keyword, triggers: eri, rewind, rewind quirk, reverse state, overhaul, rewind bullet, restoration -->
Eri's Quirk (Rewind) allows her to reverse a living target to a previous state — healing injuries, restoring lost Quirks, and (if uncontrolled) rewinding the target to before they existed (killing them). The Quirk is one of the most powerful in the series and is treated as a world-class strategic asset. Overhaul used it to make the Quirk-erasing drug (Rewind a target's body to a state before they had a Quirk). Eri does not fully control it — she rewound her father to nothing as a child (accidental, uncontrolled), which was the source of the Hassaikai's interest in her. Post-Overhaul-arc, she's in U.A.'s care (Aizawa is her primary caregiver). Her Quirk can restore One For All (if Izuku's body is destroyed by 100% usage, Eri can rewind him). The Quirk is the one exception to "death is final" — Rewind can, in theory, reverse a death if applied immediately.

## 6. ECONOMY

### ECONOMY -- Yen and the Hero Salary System
<!-- rag: keyword, triggers: yen, hero salary, hero pay, hero income, hero commission, hero ranking pay -->
**Currency:** Yen (¥). Modern Japanese economy.
**Hero Salary Structure:** Licensed heroes are paid by the HPSC on a salary + bonus structure. Base salary scales with billboard rank: top-10 heroes earn ¥50-100M+ annually (salary + sponsorships + merch); top-100 heroes earn ¥10-30M; licensed heroes ¥5-15M; sidekicks ¥3-8M (paid by their agency, not the HPSC). Bonuses for incident resolution scale with threat level (D-rank incident ¥50-200K bonus; S-rank ¥10M+). Sponsorships and merchandise (hero-branded products, costumes, action figures) are a major income stream for top heroes — Endeavor's merch revenue exceeds his HPSC salary. Hero agencies are businesses: they earn from HPSC dispatch fees, sponsorships, and merch, and they pay sidekicks and work-study students from that revenue.
**Cost Anchors:** Bowl of ramen ¥600-1200; U.A. uniform ¥15,000; hero costume (basic) ¥500,000-2M; support gear item ¥200,000-1M; apartment in Musutafu ¥80,000-150,000/month; hero agency office rent ¥2-5M/month.
**Income Anchors:** Civilian salaryman ¥4-6M/year; U.A. student stipend ¥0 (unpaid — students pay tuition); sidekick ¥3-8M/year; licensed hero ¥5-15M/year; top-10 hero ¥50-100M+/year (salary + sponsorships).

### ECONOMY -- Support Gear and the Hero Equipment Market
<!-- rag: keyword, triggers: support gear market, hero equipment, hatsume, support course, hero industry, support company -->
The support gear industry is ~8% of the hero equipment budget — small in absolute terms but strategically critical. Major companies (Detnerat — Re-Destro's former company, before it was revealed as the MLA's corporate arm) manufacture support gear at scale; independent inventors (Mei Hatsume) produce custom gear for specific heroes. The industry is regulated; custom gear for unlicensed individuals is illegal. The black market for support gear (Quirk-amplifying gear without certification, military-grade equipment) exists but is small compared to the Quirk-erasing drug market.

### ECONOMY -- The Black Market and the Quirk-erasing Drug
<!-- rag: keyword, triggers: black market, quirk erasing drug, quirk trafficking, support gear black market, villain economy -->
Every major city has a function grey/black market. Common black market goods: Quirk-erasing drugs (Overhaul's product — rare, dangerous, but samples circulate post-Overhaul-arc), Quirk-trafficking (rare; mostly AFO's old network), support gear (uncertified amplifiers), intelligence on hero patrol routes (valuable to villains), and Quirk-supremacist literature (MLA material, banned but circulated). The PLF's corporate apparatus (Detnerat, before its exposure) was a major legitimate front for the MLA's economy. Post-PLF War, the villain economy is fractured — the PLF's funding was disrupted, but the Tartarus breakout flooded the market with high-tier villains willing to pay for gear and drugs.

## 7. EVENTS

### EVENT -- The Dawn of Quirks (~200 years ago)
<!-- rag: keyword, triggers: dawn of quirks, luminescent baby, first quirk, quirk origin, afo origin, dark age -->
The first recorded Quirk manifested in a "luminescent baby" in Qing Qing, China. Within a generation, Quirks spread globally. The initial chaos was immense — existing legal, military, and social structures were not designed for superpowers. This chaos birthed All For One (the Quirk to steal and redistribute Quirks), who unified Japan's criminal underworld during the dark age. The hero system was created in response — to license and regulate those who fought back. The Dawn of Quirks is the origin point of the modern world.

### EVENT -- All Might's Debut and the First All For One Defeat (~60 years ago)
<!-- rag: keyword, triggers: all might debut, first afo defeat, nana shimura, all might origin, symbol of peace origin -->
All Might (Toshinori Yagi) was Nana Shimura's (the 7th One For All holder) student. He debuted as a hero and became the Symbol of Peace. The first fight with All For One (~5-6 years before canon) cost All Might half his respiratory system and forced him to time-limit his hero work; he kept the injury secret from the public. He defeated AFO (imprisoned him in Tartarus) and held the international villainy rate down by existing for ~40 years. All Might's career is the golden age of hero society — his retirement is its test.

### EVENT -- The U.S.J. Attack (Year 1 of canon)
<!-- rag: keyword, triggers: usj attack, league of villains, first attack, nomu, shigaraki, all might saves, year 1 -->
The League of Villains' first public attack. Tomura Shigaraki led a small force + a Nomu to attack U.A.'s U.S.J. training facility, intending to kill All Might. All Might was not present (scheduled); Aizawa fought alone; students scattered across zones; All Might arrived and defeated the Nomu. The U.S.J. attack was the first time the League was a public threat — proof that the Symbol of Peace's retirement was already being tested.

### EVENT -- The Kamino Ward Incident (Year 1)
<!-- rag: keyword, triggers: kamino ward, all might vs afo, retirement, symbol of peace end, kamino incident -->
All Might's final battle against All For One, broadcast live on national television. All Might won, burned out the last of One For All, and retired. The Kamino Ward Incident is the moment the Symbol of Peace ended — the central pivot of the post-Kamino era. Endeavor succeeded All Might as #1 by ranking (not by public faith); the country mourned All Might and feared Endeavor. The post-Kamino era is defined by this vacuum: rising villain activity, public anxiety, the Hero Killer Stain's ideology spreading, and the League of Villains consolidating.

### EVENT -- The U.A. Training Camp Attack (Year 2)
<!-- rag: keyword, triggers: training camp attack, league of villains, bakugo kidnapped, year 2, mustafa training camp -->
The League of Villains attacked U.A.'s summer training camp, kidnapping Bakugo (attempting to recruit him — they saw his aggression as a villain asset). The attack was a PR disaster for U.A. (the camp was supposed to be secure) and led to the Heights Alliance dorm construction (students moved on-campus for security). Bakugo was rescued; the League's recruitment failed; but the attack marked the escalation of the League from a cell to a serious threat.

### EVENT -- The Overhaul Arc (Year 2)
<!-- rag: keyword, triggers: overhaul arc, shie hassaikai raid, eri rescue, sir nighteye death, quirk erasing drug -->
The Shie Hassaikai raid: heroes (Sir Nighteye Agency work-studies + U.A. students) assaulted the Yakuza compound to rescue Eri (the child whose Rewind Quirk was being used to make the Quirk-erasing drug). Sir Nighteye was mortally wounded and died in hospital. Eri was rescued; Overhaul was captured and his arms severed by Shigaraki (punishment for "disrespecting the League"). The raid was a tactical success but a strategic loss — Sir Nighteye's death was the series' first major hero death. The Quirk-erasing drug samples entered the wild.

### EVENT -- The Meta Liberation Army Arc (Year 2)
<!-- rag: keyword, triggers: mla arc, deika city, shigaraki awakening, redestro, liberation army, plf origin -->
Shigaraki's League attacked the Meta Liberation Army in Deika City (the MLA's corporate-political base). Shigaraki killed ~100 MLA soldiers, defeated Re-Destro (the MLA leader), and Re-Destro bent the knee. The merger of the League and the MLA created the Paranormal Liberation Front (PLF) — Shigaraki as Grand Commander, Re-Destro as Lieutenant Commander. The MLA arc was Shigaraki's Awakening (his Decay evolved to area-of-effect). The PLF was born: ~116,000 personnel, a small army, with the MLA's corporate and political apparatus funding it.

### EVENT -- The Paranormal Liberation War (Year 2-3)
<!-- rag: keyword, triggers: paranormal liberation war, plf war, jaku city, gunga mountain, hawks kills twice, tartarus breakout, dabi broadcast -->
The PLF War opened with a combined hero assault on the PLF: a strike on the Jaku General Hospital (Dr. Kyudai's Nomu lab) and a strike on the Gunga Mountain Villa (PLF HQ). The Jaku strike triggered Shigaraki's Awakening (Decay leveled several city blocks, killing thousands). The Gunga strike captured ~16,000 PLF soldiers but Hawks killed Twice (the war's most morally charged moment). Dabi broadcast his identity (Toya Todoroki) on live television, revealing the Todoroki family's history. All For One's coded signal triggered the Tartarus breakout, releasing him and hundreds of high-risk villains. The PLF War was the moment the post-Kamino order collapsed — the worst villains in Japanese history were back on the street, the hero society's reputation was in free fall, and the Todoroki family's wound was public.

### EVENT -- The Tartarus Breakout (during PLF War)
<!-- rag: keyword, triggers: tartarus breakout, afo escape, prison break, high risk villains, plf war -->
All For One's coded signal (broadcast during the PLF War) triggered a coordinated breakout at Tartarus and other prisons. AFO escaped, along with hundreds of high-risk villains (including Hero Killer Stain, Lady Nagant, and many others). The Tartarus Breakout is the moment the hero society's containment system failed catastrophically — the worst villains in Japanese history were back on the street within hours. Post-breakout, the hero society is in a state of emergency; many escapees remain at large.

### EVENT -- Star and Stripe's Death (Final War)
<!-- rag: keyword, triggers: star and stripe death, cathleen bate, new order, shigaraki afo, final war opening -->
Star and Stripe (Cathleen Bate, the US #1 hero) came to Japan to fight Shigaraki-AFO when she heard All Might's successor needed help. She used New Order to rewrite her own body's rules after being destroyed, detonating her own mass to damage Shigaraki-AFO. Her death was a global event — the strongest hero in the world died fighting. She damaged Shigaraki-AFO significantly but did not kill him. Her death is the Final War's opening shock.

### EVENT -- The Final War and the Fall of All For One (Year 3)
<!-- rag: keyword, triggers: final war, afo defeat, shigaraki defeat, izuku vs shigaraki, end of series, final battle -->
The Final War: the combined heroes vs All For One, Shigaraki, and the remnants of the PLF. Izuku confronted Shigaraki inside the Decay, speaking to the Tenko who wanted to be a hero as a child — the series' most contested redemption attempt. All For One was defeated (his body-hopping was interrupted, his consciousness was destroyed). Shigaraki was defeated (Izuku reached him; Tenko emerged; Shigaraki died as himself, not as AFO's weapon). The war's cost was immense — hero casualties (Midnight, Sir Nighteye earlier, others), civilian casualties (tens of thousands), and the hero society's reputation in tatters. The Final War is the series' climax — the moment the cycle of hero-villain violence was, possibly, broken.
